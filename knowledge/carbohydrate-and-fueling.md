# Carbohydrate and Training Fuel

Carbohydrate is the body's main fuel for moderate-to-high-intensity exercise. It
is stored as glycogen in muscle and liver, and these stores are limited, so
carbohydrate availability strongly influences performance in hard or prolonged
sessions.

## How needs scale

Carbohydrate needs track training volume and intensity, not a fixed number:

- **Lower-activity days / lighter training:** lower intake is fine.
- **Hard or long endurance training:** needs rise substantially, and athletes
  doing high volumes may require several grams per kilogram of body weight daily.

## Around training

- **Before:** a carbohydrate-containing meal a few hours before hard sessions
  tops up glycogen and supports performance.
- **During:** for sessions beyond ~60–90 minutes, consuming carbohydrate can help
  maintain intensity; for short sessions it is generally unnecessary.
- **After:** carbohydrate plus protein after demanding sessions supports glycogen
  replenishment, which matters most when the next hard session is soon.

## Strength training context

Resistance sessions use less total carbohydrate than long endurance work, but
glycogen still fuels higher-volume lifting. Very low-carbohydrate intake can make
hard, high-volume sessions feel harder. Match intake to how demanding your week
actually is.

## Practical guidance

- Scale carbohydrate up on hard/long days and down on easy days.
- Prioritise carbohydrate around your most demanding sessions.
- Whole-food sources (grains, fruit, starchy vegetables) work well; convenient
  quick carbs are useful around long training.
