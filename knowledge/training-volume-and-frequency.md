# Training Volume and Frequency

**Volume** (usually counted as hard sets per muscle group per week) and
**frequency** (sessions per muscle group per week) are two of the biggest levers
for muscle growth and strength.

## Volume

For hypertrophy, a frequently cited general target is roughly **10–20 hard sets
per muscle group per week**, with many trainees doing well in the lower half of
that range and more advanced lifters tolerating more. "Hard sets" means sets
taken reasonably close to failure (see RPE). More is not always better: beyond an
individual's recoverable volume, returns diminish and fatigue accumulates.

## Frequency

Total weekly volume appears to matter more than how it is split up. Training a
muscle **2–3 times per week** lets you distribute volume into higher-quality
sessions compared with cramming everything into one. Frequency is mostly a tool
for *organising* volume and managing per-session fatigue, not an independent magic
variable.

## Strength vs. hypertrophy emphasis

- **Strength** favours heavier loads (lower reps, ~1–6) with enough practice of
  the specific lifts; frequency on the main lifts helps skill.
- **Hypertrophy** is driven by sufficient volume of challenging sets across a
  range of rep targets (roughly 5–30 reps when sets are hard).

## Practical guidance

- Start at the lower end of the volume range and add sets only as recovery allows.
- Split weekly volume across 2–3 sessions per muscle group when practical.
- Use soreness, performance, and sleep as feedback; persistent decline signals too
  much volume or too little recovery.
