# Periodization and Deloads

Periodization is the planned variation of training variables (volume, intensity,
exercise selection) across time to manage fatigue and peak performance. Rather
than doing the same thing every week, you organise training into phases with
different emphases.

## Common models

- **Linear** — volume starts high and intensity low, then gradually shifts toward
  lower volume and higher intensity over a block.
- **Undulating** — volume and intensity vary within the week (e.g., a heavy day,
  a moderate day, a lighter day).
- **Block** — successive blocks each emphasise one quality (accumulation,
  intensification, realisation/peaking).

No single model is universally superior; the best choice depends on the trainee's
level, goals, and schedule.

## Why deload

Hard training accumulates fatigue faster than it builds fitness. Left unmanaged,
fatigue masks fitness and raises injury risk. A **deload** is a planned period of
reduced training stress that lets fatigue dissipate so the underlying fitness can
show — often leading to better performance afterward.

## How to structure a deload week

A deload typically lasts about one week and reduces training stress while keeping
movement patterns sharp. Common approaches:

- **Reduce volume** — cut working sets roughly by half while keeping load moderate.
- **Reduce intensity** — keep set count but drop load to ~60–70% of normal.
- **Reduce both modestly** — a gentler trim of each.

Frequency of deloads is individual: many intermediate lifters deload every 4–8
weeks, or when performance, sleep, motivation, or joints signal accumulated
fatigue. Autoregulation (see RPE) can replace fixed scheduling.
